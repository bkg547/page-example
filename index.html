Engineering a Phage-Delivered Decoy Protein to Combat Beta-Lactam Antibiotic Resistance

Team Information

National Tsing Hua University/Taiwan

iGEM 2025

Project Overview

Our innovative approach combines bacteriophage delivery systems with engineered decoy proteins to combat antibiotic resistance. By targeting beta-lactamase enzymes, we aim to restore the effectiveness of beta-lactam antibiotics and address one of the most pressing challenges in global health.

Project Motivation

Antibiotic resistance represents one of the most significant threats to global public health. Our project addresses the critical challenge of beta-lactam antibiotic resistance, which renders many common antibiotics ineffective.

Background

Beta-lactam antibiotics, including penicillin and amoxicillin, have been cornerstone treatments for bacterial infections. However, the rise of beta-lactamase-producing bacteria has severely compromised their effectiveness.

Project Objectives

  • Design and engineer a novel decoy protein targeting beta-lactamase enzymes
  • Develop an effective phage delivery system
  • Validate the system's ability to restore antibiotic effectiveness

Specificity & Mechanism

Our decoy protein specifically targets beta-lactamase enzymes, mimicking the beta-lactam ring structure to act as a competitive inhibitor and prevent antibiotic degradation.

Phage Delivery System

Utilizing engineered bacteriophages as delivery vehicles, we ensure targeted delivery of our decoy protein directly to resistant bacteria.

Protein Engineering

Advanced computational modeling and directed evolution approaches optimize our decoy protein design for maximum effectiveness.
